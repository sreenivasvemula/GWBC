Deleting, Removing, and Inactivating a Contact

In ContactManager, you can delete a contact, but you cannot inactivate a contact.
If you attempt to delete a contact in ContactManager, ContactManager checks with the other applications to see if that contact can be deleted. ContactManager deletes the contact only if all applications report that the contact can be deleted.
In the base configuration, you can remove a contact from an account if no policy or work order uses the contact, currently or on a previously bound policy transaction. You can also inactivate a contact on an account, and the contact remains on previously bound policies or policy transactions. However, you cannot add an inactive contact to new, changed, reinstated, rewritten, issued, or renewed policies. You can remove a contact on a policy. Removing or inactivating a contact on an account or policy does not delete the contact from BillingCenter.
You can configure BillingCenter to delete a contact. If the ContactManager integration is enabled and you delete a contact in BillingCenter, the default integration does not send a delete message to ContactManager.
